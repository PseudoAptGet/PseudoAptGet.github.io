Operable

Websites carry out a function, and regardless of whether that function is presenting information, or taking your credit card number because a Nigerian prince really needs some dough, everyone should be able to use the website for its intended function (because after all, even professionals have standards). This section will go over some of the important standards, and I will try my best to implement it.

Keyboard Functionality

WCAG standards recommend that the keyboard be useful for navigation and filling out forms. To that end, one should be able to tab from one form entry slot to another, and also use the keyboard to navigate around. I tried to implement that here. Try tabbing around on the form.

Time Considerations

There should be sufficient time for one to fill out their forms, and consume whatever content is on the website. The user should be able to

  • Turn off the timer at their convenience.
  • Adjust the timer to at least ten times the default amount of time granted.
  • Extend the time (to a reasonable degree) with a quick input like pressing spacebar.

No Seizures

This one is somewhat self explanatory. There should not be content that may trigger a seizure, and the official metric is to not have any element that flashes more than three times a second.
